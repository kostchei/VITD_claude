# Wastes — Weather, Encounters & Curiosities

A summary of the "Wastes" page from *The Vast in the Dark*. Where
[generating-the-vast.md](generating-the-vast.md) stocks the map with **static**
terrain and [roaming-hazards.md](roaming-hazards.md) adds **mobile** dangers that
wander the map, this page drives **what happens day to day** while Travelers
cross the open Wastes — the weather overhead and whoever (or whatever) they run
into.

> Flavour: *vast swathes of dust and sand, punctuated by crumbling foundations
> and collapsed ceiling. Travelers are quick to leave it behind, as they are
> prone to freak, dangerous storms.*

**The daily roll:** for **every day spent in the Wastes**, roll **2d6** on the
Weather table and **1d12 + 1d6** on the Encounters table. Some weather results
modify the encounter roll or the day's travel — so resolve **weather first**.

---

## 1. 2d6 Weather

Rolled once per day. Note the two results that feed forward into other systems:
**Pillar Fog** adds `+6` to that day's encounter roll, and several results cut
the day's travel distance (base daily travel is **18 miles**; see §4).

| 2d6 | Weather | Effect |
|-----|---------|--------|
| 2–6 | **Calm** | Nothing but chill gusts of wind and the settling of dust. |
| 7 | **Dust Storm** | Dust whipped into a blinding frenzy. Travel **reduced by 6 miles**; impossible to make out landmarks. |
| 8 | **Wind Blast** | Vicious wind. Unprotected lights/fires blown out; any Traveler caught in the open risks being blown away for **3d6** damage. |
| 9 | **Stone Hail** | The ceiling rumbles and stone rains down. Unprotected Travelers **Save v. Breath** or suffer **3d6** damage. |
| 10 | **Pillar Fog** | A chill, ominous mist. **Add +6 to the encounter roll** for the day; impossible to make out landmarks. |
| 11 | **Grit Slide** | Dunes shift and collapse like avalanches. **Save v. Breath** or suffer **3d6** damage and **lose 6 miles** of progress. |
| 12 | **Dune Wave** | A tectonic shift sends waves across the wastes. Every Traveler gains a level of **exhaustion** (running from the wave) or is buried. |

> Note: `2–6` all read as **Calm** — i.e. the common case is "nothing happens."

---

## 2. Encounters — roll 1d12 + 1d6 (range 2–18)

Rolled once per day, **after** weather. The encounter result is **`1d12 + 1d6`**,
so the table is read on **2–18** (row 1 is unreachable). The **same `1d6` is the
Mood die** — for entries that have a disposition, read that second die against
their Mood line. **Pillar Fog** adds a further `+6` on top, pushing the roll
toward the worse monster rows.

| 2–18 | Encounter | Detail / Mood (the 1d6 you rolled) |
|------|-----------|------------------------------------|
| 1–5 | **Nothing** | You are alone, for now. (2–5 are the reachable "nothing" rows.) |
| 6 | **1d6 Lost Travelers** | Desperate for food and shelter; helpful if assisted. |
| 7 | **1d6 Nomads** | Braving the weather. **Mood:** `1` Cautious (hostile if disturbed) · `2–4` Curious (peaceful if hailed) · `5–6` Friendly (gives directions, warns of dangers). |
| 8 | **1d3 Merchants** | Wares on a heavy pulk; will trade/buy/sell. Limit **100 coin**. |
| 9 | **1d6 Bandits** | Prowling for an easy score. **Mood:** `1–2` Crazed (attack to kill & loot) · `3–5` Tribute (demand 100 coin or a ration each) · `6` Curious (let characters join a raid). |
| 10 | **2d6 Pilgrims** | Bound for the nearest ruin/pillar; devoted to a random faction. Friendly if joined, hostile if stopped. |
| 11 | **1d6 Lodestone Prospectors** | Hauling **1d20 raw lodestone** (pg. 12) on a sleigh. Cautious; hostile if harassed. |
| 12 | **Caravan: 1d6 Merchants + 2d6 Nomads** | Wares on a heavy pulk; trade/buy/sell. Limit **1000 coin**. |
| 13 | **1d6 Cutthroats** | Out for blood and plunder. **Mood:** `1–3` Crazed (kill & loot) · `4–5` Tribute (100 coin or all rations) · `6` Recruit (characters fight; survivor joins them). |
| 14 | **1d6 Cyclops** | Clustered for warmth, smelling the air for unwary mortals. |
| 15 | **1d3 Harpies** | Circling above or buried under the dust, patiently waiting. |
| 16 | **1d3 Medusa** | Lurking in corners and hidden spots, listening for footsteps. |
| 17 | **Shade** | Drifting through the air and across the dust, vibrating with hunger. |
| 18 | **Griffon** | Resting on the highest point; the remains of its latest victim lie nearby. |

> The added `1d6` already biases the daily roll toward the middle of the table;
> **Pillar Fog's `+6`** stacks on top and is what reliably reaches the monster
> rows (`14–18`) — bad weather literally draws out worse things.

## 3. 1d20 Curiosities

The page's third table — points of interest found while crossing the Wastes
(rolled when the GM wants a landmark, or on a "discovery" result). Several
provide **shelter** (relevant against the weather above) or yield loot/rations.

| 1d20 | Curiosity |
|------|-----------|
| 1 | **Ruin outcropping** — provides shelter; **1-in-6** chance of an encounter. |
| 2 | **Abandoned camp** — signs of violence; **1d3 rations** and random loot. |
| 3 | **Stone totem** of geometric design; offerings/effigies at its base. |
| 4 | **Desiccated corpses** of nomads; one utters a single word before expiring. |
| 5 | **Burial cairn**, partially buried — loot for a random weapon and tool. |
| 6 | **Cache of 1d10×6 lodestone**, hidden under a stone with an illegible note. |
| 7 | **Nomad in black**, silent and still; a hand points to the nearest pillar. |
| 8 | **Collapsed tower** — all that remains of a vast ruin; provides shelter. |
| 9 | **Lodestone obelisk**, etched with symbols — mine for **1d20 raw lodestone**. |
| 10 | **Traveler tied to a pillar** — helpful if freed; no memory of before. |
| 11 | **Unearthed road** — leads to nearest ruins; counts as a landmark. |
| 12 | **Swarm** of stinging insects buffeted by the wind; **1d3 rations**. |
| 13 | **Lonely graves**, carefully laid; the bodies obscured by sand. |
| 14 | **Nest** — eyeless worms / small crabs burrow in the soft dust; **1d6 rations**. |
| 15 | **Plague-ridden corpse** — a mad ravager, mouth foaming. |
| 16 | **Secret tunnel** — provides shelter; travels **1d6 miles** in a random direction. |
| 17 | **Message** carved into a stone slab — illegible from wear and weather. |
| 18 | **Lost caravan** — corpses and goods buried by dust; **1d20 rations** or loot. |
| 19 | **Bereft Swordsman**, blade nearly rust; only says *"There is no way out."* |
| 20 | **Forgotten treasure**, buried and lost — roll **Treasure** (pg. 29). |

## 4. Movement & survival

The daily roll above is the *consequence* of advancing a day; movement and upkeep
are what *spend* the day. Per day (24 hours) on foot:

- **Base travel:** Travelers average **18 miles/day** before needing rest.
- **Forced march:** spend **a level of exhaustion** to travel an extra **6 miles**
  (so 24 miles for one exhaustion). The rules don't cap this, but each level
  stacks — exhaustion is the cost.
- **Rations:** **1 ration per Traveler per day**, or that Traveler gains **a level
  of exhaustion**. (Several Curiosities and trade encounters are how rations get
  topped up.)
- **Terrain drives the tables:** weather and encounters are rolled **on the table
  for the hex's terrain**. The tables in §1–§3 are the **Wastes** tables; Ruins,
  Settlements, and Pillars use their own (not on this page). So advancing a day
  means: pick the table by current terrain, then roll on it.

Note this reconciles the "6 miles" figures in the Weather table: those are
subtractions from the **18-mile** base (Dust Storm and Grit Slide each knock off
6, down to 12), not the whole budget.

## 5. Pseudocode

```text
# ----- Tables -------------------------------------------------------------

WEATHER(roll):                            # 2d6
    if roll in 2..6:  return CALM         # the common "nothing" case
    if roll == 7:     return DUST_STORM
    if roll == 8:     return WIND_BLAST
    if roll == 9:     return STONE_HAIL
    if roll == 10:    return PILLAR_FOG
    if roll == 11:    return GRIT_SLIDE
    if roll == 12:    return DUNE_WAVE

ENCOUNTER(roll):                          # roll = 1d12 + 1d6 (+ mods); 2..18+
    if roll in 1..5:  return NOTHING
    if roll == 6:     return LOST_TRAVELERS        # 1d6
    if roll == 7:     return NOMADS                # 1d6  + mood
    if roll == 8:     return MERCHANTS             # 1d3,  limit 100
    if roll == 9:     return BANDITS               # 1d6  + mood
    if roll == 10:    return PILGRIMS              # 2d6
    if roll == 11:    return LODESTONE_PROSPECTORS # 1d6, 1d20 lodestone
    if roll == 12:    return CARAVAN               # 1d6 merchants + 2d6 nomads
    if roll == 13:    return CUTTHROATS            # 1d6  + mood
    if roll == 14:    return CYCLOPS               # 1d6
    if roll == 15:    return HARPIES               # 1d3
    if roll == 16:    return MEDUSA                # 1d3
    if roll == 17:    return SHADE
    if roll >= 18:    return GRIFFON

CURIOSITY(roll):                          # 1d20 -> table row above
    return CURIOSITY_TABLE[roll]

BASE_MILES_PER_DAY = 18                    # on foot, before needing rest
FORCED_MARCH_MILES = 6                     # extra distance per exhaustion level

# ----- Survival upkeep (per Traveler, per day) ----------------------------

upkeep(party):
    for t in party:
        if t.rations > 0:                  # 1 ration consumed per day...
            t.rations -= 1
        else:                              # ...or a level of exhaustion
            t.exhaustion += 1

# ----- One day on the map -------------------------------------------------
# Tables are TERRAIN-KEYED. This page covers WASTES; other terrains supply
# their own WEATHER/ENCOUNTER tables (selected the same way).
# Resolve weather first: it can cut travel and modify the encounter roll.

spend_day(party, hex, push_extra_marches = 0):
    tables = TABLES_FOR(hex.terrain)       # WASTES tables for a Wastes hex
    travel_miles  = BASE_MILES_PER_DAY
    encounter_mod = 0

    # --- Forced march: buy extra distance with exhaustion ---
    for i in 1..push_extra_marches:
        travel_miles += FORCED_MARCH_MILES
        for t in party:  t.exhaustion += 1

    # --- Weather (on this terrain's table) ---
    weather = tables.WEATHER(random 2d6)
    switch weather:
        CALM:        pass
        DUST_STORM:  travel_miles -= 6;  party.lose_landmarks()
        WIND_BLAST:
            party.extinguish_unprotected_lights()
            for t in party.exposed:  t.risk_blown_away(3d6)
        STONE_HAIL:
            for t in party.unprotected:  if not save(BREATH): t.take(3d6)
        PILLAR_FOG:  encounter_mod += 6;  party.lose_landmarks()
        GRIT_SLIDE:
            for t in party:  if not save(BREATH): t.take(3d6)
            travel_miles -= 6
        DUNE_WAVE:
            for t in party:  t.exhaustion += 1   # running, else buried

    # --- Encounter (on this terrain's table) ---
    # ONE pair of dice: d12 sets the row, d6 both bumps the row AND is the mood.
    d12 = random 1..12
    d6  = random 1..6                     # the Mood die, reused below
    enc = tables.ENCOUNTER(d12 + d6 + encounter_mod)
    if enc != NOTHING:
        group = roll_group_size(enc)      # e.g. 1d6, 1d3, 2d6 ...
        mood  = MOOD(enc, d6) if enc has_mood else none   # same d6 as above
        resolve_encounter(party, enc, group, mood)

    # --- Upkeep: eat or tire ---
    upkeep(party)

    return max(travel_miles, 0)           # miles actually advanced this day

# ----- Mood (1d6) for encounters that have one ----------------------------

MOOD(enc, roll):
    if enc == NOMADS:
        if roll == 1:     return CAUTIOUS   # hostile if disturbed
        if roll in 2..4:  return CURIOUS    # peaceful if hailed
        if roll in 5..6:  return FRIENDLY
    if enc == BANDITS:
        if roll in 1..2:  return CRAZED     # kill & loot
        if roll in 3..5:  return TRIBUTE    # 100 coin / ration each
        if roll == 6:     return CURIOUS    # join a raid
    if enc == CUTTHROATS:
        if roll in 1..3:  return CRAZED     # kill & loot
        if roll in 4..5:  return TRIBUTE    # 100 coin / all rations
        if roll == 6:     return RECRUIT    # survivor joins them
```

## 6. Notes for implementation

- **The encounter roll is `1d12 + 1d6` (2–18), not `1d12`.** Roll the pair
  together: the `d12` sets the base row, the `d6` is added to it **and** is the
  Mood die — so one throw gives both the encounter and its disposition. Don't
  clamp the sum; **Pillar Fog**'s `+6` stacks on top to reach the monster rows.
- **Order matters:** roll weather before encounters, since Pillar Fog feeds
  `encounter_mod`.
- **Travel** is a per-day budget starting at **18 miles**; Dust Storm and Grit
  Slide subtract **6** each. Floor at 0.
- **Forced march:** every extra **6 miles** costs the whole party **a level of
  exhaustion**; model it as a caller-chosen `push_extra_marches` count, not an
  automatic thing.
- **Ration upkeep:** charge **1 ration per Traveler per day**; anyone who can't
  pay gains **a level of exhaustion**. Run this once per day regardless of
  weather/encounter outcome.
- **Tables are terrain-keyed.** `spend_day` selects `WEATHER`/`ENCOUNTER` via
  `TABLES_FOR(hex.terrain)`. The tables on this page are the **Wastes** set;
  Ruins / Settlements / Pillars hexes need their own (other pages). Keep the day
  routine terrain-agnostic and swap the table object.
- "X-in-6" and "Save v. Breath/Charm" reuse the same mechanics as
  [roaming-hazards.md](roaming-hazards.md) — share one `save(stat)` /
  `roll_x_in_6(x)` helper across both systems.
- Encounters carry a **group size** (its own dice: `1d6`, `1d3`, `2d6`, …) and
  sometimes a **mood** (`1d6`); keep these as data on the encounter entry rather
  than hard-coding per call site.
- **Curiosities** are a separate `1d20` table, not part of the daily auto-roll —
  surface them as landmarks/discoveries. Several grant **shelter** (the counter
  to harmful weather) or **rations** (the counter to the daily upkeep cost).
